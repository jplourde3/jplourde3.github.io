What is Linux?

Linux is actually the kernal of a full operating system the full name being GNU/Linux. For simplicities sake, people just call it Linux. When looking into the Linux operating system, you may hear many different names of "distributions", some expamples being Ubuntu, Arch, Debian, Pop! OS, Linux Mint, and many more. These are all different versions of Linux as a whole. Each one is a twist on the same operating system. As a whole, much of Linux is similar between these different "twists". People use Linux for its customization and it being open source. Open source means that many of the programs that you will run into while using the operating system (and the operating system itself) are free to use, change, or anything else your heart desires. This does not mean all programs, but a majority of the popular programs within Linux are open source. Another reason why many people and companies use Linux is because of its security. Because so many people look at the code whenever a new update comes out, because of its open source nature, many bugs and security holes are fixed before people with bad intentions are able to find loopholes in the code. Due to a small market share, many popular programs have not come to Linux yet. In the future, that may change as popularity is growing slowly.

Pros and Cons

Benefits of Linux

  • Very customizable
  • Open sourced
  • Choose from a variety of operating systems within Linux
  • Secure
  • Very few trackers due to no one company controlling it

Downsides of Linux

  • Many popular programs not usable with Linux yet (Microsoft Word, Adobe Photoshop, etc.)
  • No singular resource for support
  • Some distributions are complex to install
  • Difficult to find a computer with Linux pre-installed
  • May have to use the terminal for some tasks, depending on the distribution

Who Should Use It?

  • Very popular among programmers
  • People that like to customize their workspace
  • People who are worried about their data being tracked by large companies

What Distribution Should I Get?

Many people want to try Linux but are overwhelemed by the amount of distributions that are available to download. Here is a short List of where one could start for a well supported, easy to use version of Linux.

  • Ubuntu
  • Pop! OS
  • Linux Mint
  • Elementary OS (if switching from Mac OS)
